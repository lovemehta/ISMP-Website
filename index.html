Institute Student Mentorship Programme

Institute Student Mentorship Program (ISMP) is a student initiative of IIT Ropar, set up to provide a comprehensive and in-depth knowledge to freshmen on critical issues like academics, co and extra-curricular activities and how to bring about an optimum balance between them which proves decisive in shaping an individual's contentment levels in the institute. To ensure this, every fresher is allotted a mentor who obviously is a senior student with substantial experience in plethora of matters to guide the freshmen through the transition process. The mentors are selected through a rigorous process to make sure they can guide mentees to take informed decisions in all spheres.

They can be approached at any time of the day either by personally meeting them or by virtual means. Also every mentor maintains a group in Whatsapp or Facebook to ensure that maximum interaction is possible with little hesitation. Mentors will guide all the mentees throughout the year and it is where the latter get to learn a lot and become mentors in the forthcoming years

'IITs have to produce leaders not just engineers' - Prof. Sarit Kumar Das appointed as the new director of IIT Ropar

"There has been a marked shift in the mandate of Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) from producing engineering workforce to the new role of preparing leaders," says our newly arrived director sir, adding these leaders would go on to become top academicians, company executives and entrepreneurs.
New campus top priority- Shifting to the new campus at the earliest will be his first priority as the project is running behind schedule. "I will try to accelerate the process and we should move to the new campus by the end of 2016 or summer of 2017. Other new IITs, including the one in Mandi, have already got their own buildings and this is something that needs to be expedited," he adds. Putting in place a proper administrative set up for delegation of power at various levels is also being worked out, he points out.
Attracting global talent- "Attracting quality faculty has been a challenge for the new IITs and IIMs and IIT Ropar is no exception. "We presently have 66 faculty members against the sanctioned strength of 90 and it will be ensured that the vacant posts are filled up without compromising on quality. My ambition is to make IIT Ropar a world class institute," the director says.
